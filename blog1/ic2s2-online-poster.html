Exposure and Engagement: Analyzing the Impact of Visual Strategies by Fitness Influencers on Social Media

Wenchang Wang

Peking University HSBC Business School

Introduction & Background

Research Context:

Fitness influencers have significantly transformed public health behaviors through social media platforms. They share workout videos and promote healthy lifestyles, creating substantial impact on audience health choices.

However, the inherent nature of fitness videos often requires skin exposure to properly demonstrate exercise movements. This creates a complex dynamic where educational content can easily shift towards soft-porn content (Ahrens et al., 2022).

Theoretical Framework:

Media Events Theory: Dayan and Katz (1992) delineate media events as "history televised live," where broadcasting affords audiences collective and real-time viewing experiences, forging shared visibility within society.

Visibility & Power: The live broadcasting of media events is often intertwined with power structures. Through social platform systems, influencers engage in commercial exploitation of their own value (Uldam, 2018).

Attention Economy: Influencers must contemplate content strategies that counter traffic distribution tactics of social media platforms, vying for visibility in an era where attention has become a scarce resource.

Literature Review

Previous Visual Studies Limitations:

  • Manual Classification: Small samples and slow processing speed (Ahrens, 2022)
  • Unsupervised Learning: Methods lack theoretical explanatory power (Peng, 2021; Peng et al., 2023; Zhang & Peng, 2024)
  • Scale Issues: Previous studies limited by methodological constraints

Research Gaps:

  • Systematic analysis of gender differences in body exposure strategies
  • Large-scale quantitative assessment of engagement patterns
  • Temporal evolution of content strategies
  • Platform-specific visibility management tactics

Research Questions & Hypotheses

RQ1: Visual Presentation Strategies

Question: Are there systematic differences in the utilization of body exposure strategies between male and female fitness content creators?

Context:

  • Female Patterns: Sexualization through sexy poses and facial expressions vs. "keeping it raw" with authentic body photos and real conversations about body image
  • Male Patterns: Metrosexual style exploring diverse masculine expressions, though not comparable to female objectification levels

RQ2: Interaction Feedback Mechanisms

Question: Is there a significant association between the degree of body exposure in content and user engagement metrics (likes and comments)?

Engagement Triggers:

  • Safety Topics: Personal and public safety content leads to higher engagement
  • Ethnic Emotion: National identity content evokes emotional resonance
  • Social Justice: Gender equality and racial justice discussions prompt active engagement
  • Provocative Content: Sensual content attracts significant attention and comments

RQ3: Operational Strategy Evolution

Question: Does the blogger's follower base size influence their adoption of body exposure strategies?

Strategic Considerations:

  • Visibility Management: Complex process encompassing self-monitoring, content strategy, social interaction, and brand collaboration
  • Micro-influencer Advantage: Closer-knit fan communities and frequent interactions (van der Harst & Angelopoulos, 2024)
  • Risk Management: Sexual content creators limit visibility through strategic concealment and platform-specific posting (Stegeman & Poell, 2024)

Results & Analysis

Universal Presence: 100% of accounts (n=50) contained at least one algorithmically classified suggestive content image.

Gender Differences: Significant difference between males and females (Chi-square statistic: 4401.763, p<0.001). Contrary to expectations, males are more likely to post soft porn images than females.

Key Findings:

  • Soft porn images significantly reduce comment numbers
  • Threshold effect for likes - quantity matters more than mere presence
  • Mann-Whitney U tests confirmed significant engagement differences (p<0.001)

Strategic Evolution Pattern

Early stage influencers use body exposure as visibility competition strategy, but as influence grows and advertising revenue becomes important, high-quality interactions (comments) become more valuable than pure engagement numbers (likes).

Conclusions

Three-Level Analysis Summary:

1. Image-Level: Universal presence of suggestive content across all fitness influencer accounts, with unexpected gender patterns.

2. Post-Level: Clear trade-off between engagement quantity (likes) and quality (comments) based on content strategy.

3. Account-Level: Strategic evolution from visibility-seeking through exposure to quality engagement focus as influence grows.

Theoretical Implications

The study reveals how visibility management in health communication involves complex strategic calculations balancing immediate attention capture with long-term brand sustainability.

Contributions

Methodological Innovation:

  • Computer vision model for health communication research
  • Faster and more effective than manual classification
  • Clearer standards and greater accuracy than cluster analysis
  • Naturalistic data with better temporal extension and ecological validity

Theoretical Advancement:

  • Enriched visibility theory in health communication
  • Platform factor integration in health communication studies
  • Alternative direction for practical implementation

Practical Implications:

  • Challenges one-sided praise of fitness influencers
  • Big data validation of previous qualitative findings
  • Informs platform policy and content moderation strategies

Limitations & Future Research

Study Limitations:

  • Weibo's content management mechanism limits historical data access for some accounts
  • Cultural and platform-specific findings may not generalize globally
  • Binary classification may oversimplify content complexity

Future Directions:

  • Cross-platform comparative analysis
  • Longitudinal audience response studies
  • Cultural variation in visibility strategies
  • Policy intervention effectiveness assessment
